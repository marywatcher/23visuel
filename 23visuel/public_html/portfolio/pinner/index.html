Pinner app

meri@23visuel.com

problem

Client wanted to own a product that would help people connect based on two criteria: Distance and Activity. The app would give accessibility to friends and, optionally, to strangers, too.
Karen from Bright8Media and I teamed up to work on the design challenge and deliver results to developers team on client's side.

Solution (cont'd)

Social mode helps users meet people they don't know by making them publicly visible. This mode can be turned on and off by user.
Yet to give some level of privacy to the user, we came up with a feature for the user to setup (1) a timer for visiblity, as well as (2) a radius within which he/she would be publicly visible. So that, in case the user forgot to turn "Social mode" off, he/she would know it'd turn off automatically.

Meeting mode enables user to setup a meeting and invite friends. To start, the user should choose a location on the map, then invite friends and setup a radius to share the event. Although the last two steps sound conflicting, the decision was made together with the client to enable both of the features, so that users who happen to be far away from the lcoation of the meeting are not bothered by events they are not able to attend. In other words, this was a compromise in order to increase conversion rate of meeting attendance.
The user would then have a list view of all the meetings with "today's meetings" highlighted, and an option to filter meetings sent by me.

GeoMessage is the winner mode for the app as it gamifies Pinner app and pushes towards healthier choices. The way it works is a user leaves a message to one of his/her friends and the message is locked for the friend (the friend would get notification but wouldn't be able to read it) unless the friend arrives to the location (± error margin).

Process

We have had several brainstorm sessions with the client. After every session I worked on paper wireframes and then walked client through the user flow. Once we were agreed on the flow, I switched to Photoshop to work on hi-fi prototypes.
Karen and I developed the visual look and feel for the app, prepared assets for production, including buttons, icons and redlines.
